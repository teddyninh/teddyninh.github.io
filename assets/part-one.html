PART ONE
INT. FRONT GATES - DAY
A beautiful, red-brick campus is lightly covered in snow. A
sign reads “HIVERNALL UNIVERSITY - MAIN GATE”.
Imagine Harvard. Now imagine a school even more flashy and
self-important-- that’s Hivernall.
MUNDAY OENG (20s, unkempt) stands with HUNTER NEWELL (20s,
heavily-groomed). Both wear expensive tweed clothing. All the
students on campus dress similarly.
HUNTER
These past two weeks have been
tragic.
MUNDAY
I know. I haven’t been able to rest
since Prof. Hahm’s death.
Munday lights a cigarette.
HUNTER
And I can’t publish this week’s
newspaper riddle anymore, Munday.
MUNDAY
The riddle? Why not?
HUNTER
I can’t publish a riddle about a
dead professor weeks after a
professor actually died on campus.
It’ll feel derivative and
unimaginative. And to think I spent
so long devising the premise...
Hunter puts his head into his hands.
MUNDAY
Our email’s flooded too. One
professor dies, and suddenly mass
hysteria. I don’t have time for
this, I’ve got response papers due.
HUNTER
I’m sorry, Munday.
2.
MUNDAY
Yeah. It’s like when there’s a car
accident up on the highway, and you
can’t get where you’re going.
HUNTER
A “first-world problem”.
MUNDAY
They’re called “first-world
problems” because they’re the
first-most important.
HUNTER
Ah. How come I never knew that?
They step through the gates.
MUNDAY
All these people freaking out over
Prof. Hahm’s death. It’s the
problem with New-England elite
colleges. We’re so isolated from
the rest of the world that when
some tragedy happens on campus,
we’re not properly equipped to
pretend it didn’t happen and/or
ignore it. We’re forced to just
experience it for what it is.
HUNTER
Dreadful. Undeserved.
MUNDAY
Hunter, if you ask me, I personally
love the weekly riddles that we
run. They’re fun, they’re
intellectually-stimulating, and in
the end there’s no reward other
than the reward of solving the
riddle itself. That’s why they’re
so popular. It’s a nice break from
all the ass-kissing, and one-
upmanship that goes on around
campus, you know?
HUNTER
Exactly: brain-food without the
layered bullshit.
MUNDAY
How are the articles doing then?
Hunter hangs his head.
3.
HUNTER
Garnered less than 5 likes on my
“Top 15 Cool & Hot Freshmen of All
Time” piece. So you tell me.
MUNDAY
Wow, that’s--
HUNTER
I’ll tell you... it’s lamentably
substandard.
MUNDAY
Yeah... Listen. How about I’ll dig
into Prof. Hahm’s death, see if I
can uncover something, or “solve
the riddle”, so to speak. Once it’s
over, life will go back to being
privileged.
HUNTER
You’d really do that for me? For
the newspaper?
MUNDAY
Mainly for the clout. I could also
squeeze this into my resume, if all
goes well.
HUNTER
Oh, that’s true. Smart.
MUNDAY
We’re going through tough times,
but we’ll get through it together.
I’ll put out this fire.
INT. CAFETERIA - DAY
Munday sits with FREDI WHITTING (20s, fashionable).
FREDI
I have to go in a bit. I’m leading
another tour soon.
MUNDAY
Fredi, before you go... Do you
think Prof. Hahm might’ve been
murdered? Instead of an accident.
Fredi is silent.
4.
MUNDAY (CONT'D)
Like, do you think one of the other
professors might have wanted to
kill Prof. Hahm? Maybe Prof. Digby?
BEGIN FLASHBACK:
INT. SCIENCE CLASSROOM - DAY - FLASHBACK - EARLIER
CHESTER DIGBY (40s, nasally, scrawny) teaches class. Munday
takes notes.
DIGBY
Before we begin... I’m sorry about
what happened to Prof. Hahm.
(beat)
Alright. My sympathy’s all used-up,
so let’s move on.
FREDI
(walking in)
Sorry I’m late. Took the elevator,
which I only do when I’m already
running late, but it always just
makes me even more late. I was--
DIGBY
Very interesting. I’m Prof. Digby,
and I was just telling your
classmates that I’m filling in on
forensics class until the school
finds a replacement for Prof. Hahm.
Fredi sits next to Munday.
DIGBY (CONT'D)
Now, I want you all to say it with
me: Forensics is...!
Silence. The students look bored.
MUNDAY
DIGBY (CONT'D)
Fun?
Terrible! Forensics is
terrible! It’s slow, and
inaccurate, and not at all
like TV portrayals.
Still, silence.
5.
DIGBY (CONT'D)
Again, I cannot reiterate how sorry
I am about Prof. Hahm’s sudden, and
likely painful, death. Um.
END FLASHBACK:
INT. CAFETERIA - DAY
MUNDAY
He was acting awkward in class.
FREDI
But Prof. Digby had no ill will
towards Prof. Hahm. He’s literally
just an awkward person.
MUNDAY
Maybe Sheriff Brownwell, then?
BEGIN FLASHBACK:
INT. SCIENCE CLASSROOM - DAY - FLASHBACK - EARLIER
DIGBY
Now class, Sheriff Brownwell-- our
guest teacher, who is also an alum--
will be performing a demonstration
for you all.
SHERIFF BROWNWELL (40s, broad) steps forward. He points at a
dummy on the floor.
SHERIFF BROWNWELL
Hey. So, pretend this is a body for
a moment. Is he dead? Or just
napping? I can’t see any bullet
entries or signs of struggle, and I
do see him breathing, which means
he could just be napping, but I’m
still just putting the pieces
together. Dead, alive, who knows.
Now, say I shot him in the head.
(shoots dummy)
Suddenly, I see it. A clear bullet
entry, fired point-blank. Hmm. Is
it a clue?
(pulling out cigarette)
At this point, I light up a
Marlboro, something I always do to
help clear out my mind and look at
the bigger picture.
6.
The Sheriff inhales the cigarette then COUGHS immediately.
SHERIFF BROWNWELL (CONT'D)
(coughing)
And I cough, because it’s the first
time I’ve ever smoked a cigarette.
(beat)
Anyways... and so the point of this
hypothetical situation is: Anything
could happen at the crime scene
that might contaminate evidence.
Forensics is often pointless, like
your new Forensics professor over
here just said. Uh-- thank you.
Sheriff Brownwell nods and steps back.
Munday fixates on a flashlight in the Lost & Found at the
back of class.
END FLASHBACK:
INT. CAFETERIA - DAY
MUNDAY
Sheriff Brownwell is certainly...
Does he have dementia? And wasn’t
Prof. Hahm his partner on a case
before he started teaching here at
Hivernall?
FREDI
No, that doesn’t make sense. The
Sheriff would’ve had nothing to
gain from Prof. Hahm’s death.
MUNDAY
I guess... Also, Fredi, did you
notice that flashlight in the
corner of the classroom, in the
lost and found? Its been there
since Prof. Hahm’s death. Weird.
FREDI
Why would I notice something as
normal as... a normal object in a
normal place?
MUNDAY
Fair. How about Dr. Eastoff? Did
she have beef with Prof. Hahm?
(MORE)
7.
MUNDAY (CONT'D)
She was real cold about his death
when I had my appointment with her.
BEGIN FLASHBACK:
INT. THERAPIST’S OFFICE - DAY - FLASHBACK - EARLIER
Munday sits across AVIVA EASTOFF (40s, brightly-dressed).
MUNDAY
Nice to meet you Dr. Eastoff. Are
you related to Myers Eastoff?
EASTOFF
That silly boy is my son, yes. Do
you know him?
MUNDAY
Yeah, he bullies me.
EASTOFF
He’s the school bully, so... yeah,
he’ll do that. And he has anxiety.
MUNDAY
Isn’t it violating patient
confidentiality to tell me that?
EASTOFF
Patient confidentiality isn’t my
approach. What’s with your name by
the way? “Munday”.
MUNDAY
My mom didn’t know many English
names when she first came here, so
she named me after “Monday” because
it’s the worst day of the week, and
that’s the first thing she thought
of when she saw me.
EASTOFF
I see. And are you ever
embarrassed, or self-conscious
about your unconventional name?
MUNDAY
Me? Oddly no, not really--
EASTOFF
Because you should be.
8.
MUNDAY
Okay, why are you my therapist
again?
EASTOFF
Not my choice. As you know, the
school mandated that every student
do weekly counseling, after that
whole episode-thing with Prof.
Hahm’s death. Especially since some
think it was actually a suicide...
Basically, nobody knows what
actually happened. Such a bother...
But do you think they raised our
pay after forcing these hordes of
new students onto us? No. So what
that means is that I’m just gonna
do the bare minimum here. And bare
minimum means I just have to make
sure you don’t kill yourself. Not
on school grounds, that is.
Elsewhere is fair game. Make sense?
MUNDAY
(ashamed)
... Yeah.
END FLASHBACK:
INT. CAFETERIA - DAY
MUNDAY
Now, there’s no wrong way to react
to death... except for hers.
FREDI
Did it seem like a motive, though?
MUNDAY
You’re right. She didn’t seem to
hate Prof. Hahm any more than she
probably hates everyone, equally.
FREDI
Wait a second...
MUNDAY
What?
FREDI
There’s a dead rat in my food.
9.
MUNDAY
FREDI (CONT'D)
(facepalm)
(dangling the rat)
Fredi, this is serious.
Look!
MUNDAY (CONT'D)
Oh. Well that’s disgusting, Fredi,
I don’t wanna look at that.
FREDI
Fine, I’ll put it back.
She buries that rat back in her food.
MUNDAY
The food’s not been up to standards
past couple of weeks. And my only
standard is “doesn’t have rats.”
Fredi chucks the tray over her shoulder. Glass SHATTERS.
MUNDAY (CONT'D)
You just broke something! What are
you, trying to get us in trouble?
FREDI
Sorry. Forgot you were on thin ice
with the admin.
BEGIN FLASHBACK:
INT. THERAPIST'S OFFICE - DAY - FLASHBACK - EARLIER
EASTOFF
It says you were on disciplinary
leave for a year. Academic
probation. What happened there?
MUNDAY
Well see, I attribute what happened
mainly to my depression, and--
EASTOFF
Ok, that sounds depressing and...
mainly depressing, so let’s talk
about something else. Let’s see...
Are you good at anything? Any
positives in your life right now?
MUNDAY
I do well in class. At least, now I
do. It’s hard to get me motivated
sometimes, but when I set my mind
to it, I can really focus...
(MORE)
10.
MUNDAY (CONT'D)
I’ve started to do a little bit of
my own digging on what happened to
Prof. Hahm, and I think--
EASTOFF
(distracted on her phone)
Hold on a sec. Oh god. There was
another overdose a few days ago.
MUNDAY
Fun fact-- the family that owns
that big pharma company also owns
the ski resorts here in Vermont. If
you can’t kill ‘em with powdery
slopes, kill ‘em with highly
addictive painkillers, right?
EASTOFF
Munday... please stay focused. But
yes, you’re right.
MUNDAY
Right. As I was saying, I’m trying
to uncover more about Prof. Hahm’s
death. Know where I might start?
EASTOFF
Interesting... Here. Let me propose
something. Do you have a quiet
place where you go when you wanna
be by yourself?
MUNDAY
The school has abandoned, creepy
tunnels that most people don’t know
about. I hangout there sometimes
when I need peace and quiet.
EASTOFF
Good. Go there. And once you crawl
into those tunnels, tell yourself,
“I’m not gonna leave here until I
get to the very bottom of this
little mystery with Prof. Hahm.”
MUNDAY
Ok. Positive mindset. And then?
EASTOFF
If you swear not to leave ‘til you
achieve your goals... odds are
you’ll be stuck in those tunnels
forever, and I’ll never have to see
you again. Make sense?
11.
MUNDAY
(ashamed again)
... Yeah.
END FLASHBACK:
INT. CAFETERIA - DAY
FREDI
Munday, I don’t think any of these
suspects make sense... Like, are
you just picking randomly?
MUNDAY
Yup. Before I go, do you wanna toss
in any suspects? You can toss in
literally anybody.
FREDI
No. Anyways, I’m off to my tour.
What about you?
INT. SECRET TUNNELS - DAY
Munday plods through the dark, ornate tunnels.
MUNDAY (V.O.)
I don’t know. Someplace quiet. I
should clear my mind.
CONTINUE
